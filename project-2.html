Designing trust through information architecture

A redesign of Etsy's iOS buyer experience — restructuring information so shoppers can find what matters faster and feel confident enough to buy.

The Challenge

Great products, hard to trust

Etsy succeeds because shoppers value uniqueness, craftsmanship, and connection to independent creators. But user interviews revealed that the mobile experience often made those very qualities difficult to discover and trust.

Evaluating products required unnecessary effort — creating uncertainty during purchase decisions and increasing the likelihood of abandonment.

Research question

How might we improve Etsy's information architecture to help users find critical information faster and build confidence throughout the shopping experience?

Shoppers struggled to find
  • Shipping timelines
  • Pricing transparency
  • Seller credibility
  • Product details
Phase 01

Understanding the Problem

User expectations vs reality

A gap between what users wanted and what they got

What users expected
  • Unique and handcrafted products
  • Transparent pricing and shipping information
  • Confidence in seller legitimacy
  • A connection to creators and small businesses
What users experienced
  • Critical decision-making details appeared below secondary content
  • Navigation patterns fragmented the browsing experience
  • Users frequently felt overwhelmed and uncertain
  • Forced to work harder than necessary to evaluate products
How I got there

Surveys & user testing

To understand where confidence broke down, I combined what shoppers said with what they actually did — surveying their priorities and pain points, then watching them move through the existing app.

Patterns surfaced quickly: people knew what they wanted, but the information they needed to feel sure rarely appeared when they needed it.

What I ran
  • User interviews with Etsy shoppers
  • Surveys on shopping priorities and pain points
  • Usability testing on the existing app
  • Task-based observation of the buyer journey

Key insight
Trust, not discovery, became the primary design problem. Etsy users are willing to invest more time and money for unique products when they feel confident in what they're buying.

02 · Navigation

Phase 03

Rebuilding the Information Architecture

Simplifying navigation around behavior

From scattered sections to one place to Discover

Related experiences were scattered across multiple destinations, forcing users to jump between sections to accomplish a single task. The redesign consolidated Shop, Explore, and For You into a unified Discover experience, and merged Messaging with Notifications to match how users actually communicate with sellers.

Additional navigation improvements
  • Favorites renamed to Collections
  • Collections moved into the profile experience
  • Simplified "You" section with clearer hierarchy
  • Reduced navigation clutter
  • Fewer competing actions in primary navigation

Phase 04

Redesign

Guided discovery

Many users knew what they wanted but struggled to determine how to search for it. To reduce "blank canvas" friction, keyword-driven search suggestions were introduced directly within the search experience.

This supported both
  • Intent-driven shoppers
  • Inspiration-driven browsers

…with immediate entry points into relevant categories and products.

Search flow examples
Restructuring the product page

Reordering the page around how people decide

Research showed three factors consistently drove purchase confidence — price, shipping, and ratings. The product page was rebuilt to surface them first and give sellers real presence, so the information people needed appeared exactly when they were evaluating.

Visual design

Modern, familiar, and built for accessibility

The visual system was updated to match current social media and e-commerce design trends, using styles that users already know from other platforms. Typography, color, and component design were refined to create a unified, accessible experience that feels natural on iOS and modern — while still keeping Etsy's warmth and character.

Visual design / UI components
Design systems as a trust tool

Predictable, not reinvented

The visual system was modernized using interaction patterns familiar from contemporary mobile and e-commerce platforms. The goal wasn't to redesign Etsy's personality — but to create a more predictable, trustworthy environment for completing purchases.

Updates included
  • Refined typography
  • Consistent component design
  • Improved accessibility
  • Cleaner visual hierarchy
  • Stronger iOS conventions
Phase 05

Validation

Rebuilding trust through clarity

Testing the redesigned flows

After completing the redesign, users worked through updated task flows. Improving structure and hierarchy significantly reduced uncertainty throughout the shopping experience.

Participants consistently reported
  • Improved navigation confidence
  • Better awareness of available features
  • Easier orientation throughout the experience
  • Faster access to decision-making information

Outcome

Impact

The result

Rather than introducing new functionality, the project focused on making existing information easier to discover, evaluate, and understand.

  • Faster product evaluation
  • Reduced cognitive load
  • Greater seller visibility
  • Increased user confidence
  • Stronger alignment with Etsy's core value proposition

What I learned from this project

The deeper I explored the product, the more I realized this wasn't a navigation problem — it was a trust problem. Users weren't struggling because Etsy lacked features. They were struggling because the information they needed to make confident decisions wasn't appearing at the right moment.

Information architecture isn't just about organization. It's about helping people feel confident enough to take action.

The most impactful design decisions often aren't new features — they're the structural changes that make existing experiences easier to understand, trust, and enjoy.
